Voting Power

This project began with a simple question: Where do votes matter most? I grew up around DC and heard countless complaints about the undemocratic nature of our electoral system. Feeling uniquely mobile approaching the end of college, I wondered where I could move such that my vote would be most impactful. Perhaps I could move to Wyoming — maybe Maine — certainly not California, where I went to school, or DC, where I was born. It is no secret that American politics overrepresent rural areas. But I wanted to know exactly where places are unequally represented, and by how much.

The first iteration of this project was done as a group assignment for a class (GEOG 176C at UCSB). I pitched the idea and got two terrifically talented colleagues (Juan and Owen) to join my team. I led the project for its three-month duration. The final product included a poster that we presented at the annual Geography Department symposium.

Afterwards, I was still curious, so I indepenedently expanded the analysis to consider every federal election since 2000. The challenge here was incorporating redistricting since where votes matter most depends on which district those votes are cast in, and that changes. Interpreting just the state of California, I gave a lightning talk at the Center for Spatial Studies annual symposium.

The final product was an interactive map. It was my first foray into Leaflet and custom open-source web maps more generally.

Aquaculture

Primary Ocean Producers was a startup aiming to cultivate Macrocystis pyrifera in the deep ocean, in partnership with Catalina Sea Ranch. We were funded by a grant from ARPA-E to grow giant kelp en masse in order to produce carbon-neutral biofuel.

My role was to site the pilot facilities off the coast of California. Along with two aquatic biologists, I developed a hierarchical suitability model for giant kelp cultivation. Among other factors, we looked at chemical availability (for nutrients), geophysical phenomena (so the kelp would be safe), and legal restrictions (so we could build the facility).
